Causes of Psychedelic Harm

1. The Knowledge Gap 100%
100% of communities affected
The Knowledge Gap ▼
  • Treating psychedelics as quick fixes
  • Misinformation spreading faster than education
  • First-timers going in completely unprepared
2. Unsafe Practitioners 100%
100% report concerns
Unsafe Practitioners ▼
  • Self-proclaimed facilitators without training
  • Facilitators crossing boundaries
  • Culturally inappropriate care
3. System Failures 83%
83% experiencing barriers
System Failures ▼
  • Criminalization preventing people from seeking help
  • No accountability systems for bad actors
  • Underground markets without labelling and testing standards
4. Vulnerable Populations at Risk 90%
90% worried about vulnerable populations
Vulnerable Populations at Risk ▼
  • Neurodivergent individuals without adapted support
  • Trauma survivors being retraumatized
  • Marginalized communities facing additional barriers

Where Do Safety Challenges Emerge?

Our research identified five critical contexts where safety challenges emerge:

🎉 At Festivals & Parties ▼

56% report contaminated substances as top concern

  • Contaminated substances
  • No quiet spaces for overwhelm
  • Peer pressure and suggestibility
  • Absent harm reduction services
🧘 Taking Psychedelics Alone ▼

65% cite no support during crisis as major risk

  • No support during crisis
  • Panic and psychological overwhelm
  • Physical safety risks
  • Inability to call for help
👥 In Small Groups ▼
  • Group emotional responses
  • Pressure to dose higher
  • Lack of clear boundaries
  • No trained crisis support
🏥 In Facilitated Sessions ▼

70% report power abuse and boundary violations

  • Power abuse and boundary violations
  • Inadequate screening for trauma/medications
  • Sexual misconduct
  • No integration support
🌿 In Traditional/Spiritual Contexts ▼
  • Cultural appropriation
  • Spiritual manipulation
  • Unsafe ceremonial practices
  • Trauma reactivation without support

Communities are Already Responding

Community leaders report various support initiatives, but critical gaps remain.

Direct Services

  • Free Narcan and fentanyl test strips
  • Drug checking at events
  • Peer support and "Zendo" tents, hotline support
  • Integration circles

Education Initiatives

  • Workshops on vetting facilitators
  • Public education and awareness on psychedelic safety
  • Dosage and interaction guides
  • Consent and boundary education

Community Building

  • Peer support networks
  • Survivor support groups
  • Word-of-mouth warning systems
  • Local society meetings

But it's not enough. Critical gaps remain.

Building the Infrastructure of Safety

Communities are clear about what they need:

Immediate Needs

  • Drug checking access at every event
  • Crisis hotlines staffed 24/7
  • Integration circles
  • Psychedelic-friendly therapists directories

Educational Revolution

  • School and university programs
  • Corporate wellness integration
  • Public awareness campaigns

Systemic Changes

  • Decriminalization
  • Standardized facilitator certification
  • Independent ethics review boards
  • Public health approach to psychedelics
